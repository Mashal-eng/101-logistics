Frequently Asked Questions

101 LOGISTICS dispatcher acts as an intermediary between Brokers and carriers. They facilitate their carrier by getting them the highest paying loads on the preferred lanes as mentioned by their carriers working for them alongside other services as well offered by the company.

Logistics employs various types of trucks, including box trucks for smaller cargo, flatbed trucks for oversized or irregularly shaped loads, refrigerated trucks for temperature-sensitive goods, and semi-trucks for long-haul transportation. Each type serves a specific purpose in the supply chain..

Tracking and monitoring shipments are crucial for ensuring timely deliveries and maintaining the security of goods. Modern technology, such as GPS and telematics, allows logistics companies to track the real-time location of trucks, monitor temperature-sensitive cargo, and respond promptly to any issues that may arise during transit.

Logistics companies adhere to strict safety and regulatory standards to protect both their drivers and the cargo. This includes complying with federal and state transportation regulations, performing routine vehicle maintenance, and providing drivers with proper training on safety procedures.

To optimize route planning and reduce transportation costs, logistics professionals use advanced route optimization software. This technology considers factors like traffic conditions, fuel efficiency, and delivery schedules to identify the most efficient routes. Additionally, consolidation of shipments and load optimization help minimize costs while maximizing efficiency in the trucking industry.
